[01/08/2023 6:13 PM] Vesi Hiisi#0000
(re @Dan / ·ûä·üÅ·ûì·ûâ·üâ·ûº : *Page 7: "It would be desirable to remove excess reserves"
Why? The more collateral the safer the stablecoin.*) 
 
 Paying out profits for dev work and initial liquidity is fine. Cant get around those, unless someone feels generous.

That does not mean  it has to go on forever. Once a fair amount of profits are paid out, then the system could slowly shift the profits to support stability. (E.g. buying own LP tokens). 

Over time, those protocol owned LP tokens would push out everyone else, because it becomes capitally inefficient. That is great for a stablecoin, because it pumps the collateral ratio higher than what competitors can afford.

As Chris points out, it might be a problem if the POL accumulates indefinitely. Not so sure about the stagnant point, but a huge POL might increase an incentive for oracle price providers to collude.

So maybe there should be some very high collateral ratio cap, after which fees approach zero, or excess is paid out. Supportive functions should maybe get a cut anyway (mining and oracle).
